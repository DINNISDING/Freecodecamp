Mariah Carey

Mariah Carey (Mariah Carey), born on March 27, 1970 in Huntington, Long Island, New York, USA, is an American female singer, songwriter, actor, record producer, and one of the "Four Queens" in Europe and America . On June 13, 1990, the first album of the same name "Mariah Carey" was released. The album produced four champion singles headed by "Vision Of Love" . In 1995, the album "Daydream" was released, which sold 7.6 million in the United States according to Nielsen SoundScan . In 1996, he won the World Music Awards for the best-selling singer in the United States. In 1999, the song "When You Believe" sung together with Whitney Houston won the 71st Academy Award for Best Movie Theme Song . In 2005, she won the Grammy Award for Best Female R&B Singer. In 2010, she participated in the film "Cherish Life" and won the Supporting Actress Award at the 14th Hollywood International Film Festival in Italy for this film . In May 2015, the best album "#1 to Infinity" was released. As of 2019, Mariah Carey has a total of 19 Billboard No. 1 singles , and has topped the Billboard Hot 100 for 84 weeks since her first single "Vision Of Love" in 1990 .
